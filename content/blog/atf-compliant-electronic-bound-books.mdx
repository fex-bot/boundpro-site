Dealers hear “ATF-compliant” on every product page. Here’s what that phrase actually points to for an electronic Acquisition & Disposition ledger.

## 27 CFR § 478.125

This is the federal regulation that defines what an A&D record must contain. The practical takeaway for software: columns and structure should mirror the regulation — manufacturer/importer, model, serial, type, caliber/gauge, receipt, and disposition — with line numbers preserved.

## ATF Ruling 2016-1

This ruling (and related guidance) is what authorizes **cloud-based** electronic bound books, with specific safeguards around integrity, retention, and access for inspection.

In BoundPro that shows up as:

- Hash-chained, immutable change history
- US-based hosting with encryption in transit and at rest
- One-click compliance packs for inspections
- Retention well beyond the 20-year 4473 archive requirement

## What software cannot do for you

Even with the best A and D Software, **the FFL holder remains responsible** for record accuracy. Tools catch deadlines and structure the book; they don’t replace judgment at the counter.
